Research

My research lies at the intersection of applied mathematics, data science, and biomedical modeling. I focus on developing mechanistic and data-driven models that connect cardiovascular and ocular dynamics, analyze physiological signals, and support clinical decision-making. I am also interested in mathematical epidemiology, including age-structured models, intervention strategies, and the integration of data analytics with infectious disease modeling. Much of my work is interdisciplinary, bringing together mathematicians, clinicians, engineers, and data scientists to address complex real-world problems.

Methods & Technical Skills

  • Mathematical modeling: ODEs, PDEs, age-structured systems, optimal control
  • Numerical methods: FEM, finite differences, stability analysis, optimization algorithms
  • Programming: Python, MATLAB, R, C/C++, Julia
  • Data analytics: statistical modeling, machine learning, visualization
  • HPC & scientific computing workflow
